The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt - Monster Trading Cards

Basilisk

"In memory of the noble knight Roderick, slain during a valiant struggle against a basilisk. Let's hope the beast choked on his bones."

  • Class: Draconid
  • Habitat: Caves, Abandoned Canal Branches
  • Weakness: Golden Oriole, Grapeshot, Aard

Contrary to popular belief, basilisks cannot turn anything to stone with their gaze. That is small comfort, however, given that their acid, venom, claws, and teeth provide them many other ways to kill.

Bruxa

"If you must travel through the woods, steer clear of any places where you can hear several different kinds of birds at once."

  • Class: Vampire
  • Habitat: Ruins, abandoned villages
  • Weakness: Moon Dust, Black Blood, Yrden

Bruxae are rare creatures, mostly living far from population centers. Those who decide to live near men avoid crowds and emerge from their shelters only at night.

Djinn

"The pitcher emitted a puff of glowing red smoke. The smoke pulsated, then gathered up into an irregular sphere floating in front of the poet's head..."

  • Class: Elemental
  • Habitat: Bottles, Lamps
  • Weakness: Dimeritium, Elementa Oil

Fighting a djinn is extraordinarily difficult. They can fling off spells in an instant that the most accomplished human mages could never cast with years of preparation. What's more, by manipulating the element of air they can summon powerful storms, hurricanes, and gales.

Doppler

"Your package? Your brother picked it up. You know, you two're damn near identical... Hey! Where are you running off to?!"

  • Class: Relict
  • Habitat: Towns and Villages
  • Weakness: Relict Oil

Dopplers have incredible intellect and cunning, as well as the ability to take on the form of any humanoid or animal. This would make dopplers supreme assasins or thieves had not nature endowed them with generous and timid spirits which make them avoid the shedding of blood at all costs.

Ghoul

"Ghouls creep and crawl at night
Eating everything in sight
In a snap they'd eat you, too
Chop you up for a ghoulish stew!"

  • Class: Necrophage
  • Habitat: Anywhere near corpses
  • Weakness: Necrophage oil

Ghouls usually feed in small groups, at times led by an alghoul. Since they delight most in the taste of fresh carcasses, they appear wherever newly-dug graves are to be found: cemeteries, crypts, catacombs and battlefields.

Sylvan

"Sylvans are cruel, greedy and treacherous. Still, I prefer them to dh'oine."

  • Class: Relict
  • Habitat: Deep Woods
  • Weakness: Relict Oil, Samum, Devil's Puffball, Igni

Sylvans and Yakshas, a kindred species, are extremely rare woodland creatures whose appearance combines traits of goats and rotund men. These beings usually pose little danger, for they limit their contact with humans to playing harmless (though often bothersome) tricks and eating crops from their fields.

Alghoul

"An alghoul's basically a badarse ghoul."

  • Class: Necrophage
  • Habitat: Battlefields, anywhere there've been corpses left to rot.
  • Weakness: Necrophage oil, Axii

During combat alghouls try risky maneuvers aimed at knocking their opponents to the ground so the others can finish the job by tearing them to shreds. Like a normal ghoul, an injured alghoul can fall into a frenzy and attack with blind fury.

Alp

"Looked like a bruxa, but that was no bruxa..."

  • Class: Vampire
  • Habitat: Towns and Villages
  • Weakness: Yrden, Moondust, Black Blood

Alps are vampires that resemble bruxae in appearance. They usually take on the form of a woman, though they can also appear as animals.They attack at night and are most active when the moon is full.

Arachasae

"N'aracche aen woed endicen [Let sleeping arachasae lie]."

  • Class: Insectoid
  • Habitat: Damp woodlands and swamps
  • Weakness: Golden Oriole, Insectoid Oil

At first glance, a stationary arachas often looks like a part of the forest undergrowth, a fact it uses to deadly advantage when hunting. It usually begins a battle by spitting venom, then tries to grab its prey with prehensile feelers in order to drag it within reach of its crushing pincers.

Arachnomorph

"The spider shall never lie down with the fly. "

  • Class: Insectoid
  • Habitat: deep dark caves
  • Weakness: Insectoid Oil, Aard, Axii,

Anyone who does come across an arachnomorph had best hope his conscience is clear and his worldly affairs are in order, for his life shall soon end, as not even the fastest man in the world can outrun them and only a few witchers can hope to slay them.

Archespore

"Looks like a flower to me. A hideous, disgusting flower."

  • Class: Cursed One
  • Habitat: Grows in the soil of places that have seen pogrom, bloody rituals or cruel murders
  • Weakness: Cursed oil, Aard, Igni

The peculiar appearance of archespores makes them practically impossible to discern amidst other plants. By the time one draws close enough to realize what they are, it is often too late to flee.

Armored Arachasae

"That's the kinda john we call an 'armored arachas.' Hard and prickly on the otuside, but get 'im undressed and everything's soft and squishy."

  • Class: Insectoid
  • Habitat Under hollow tree stumps
  • Weakness: Golden Oriole, Insectoid Oil

An arachas' only weakness is its soft, sensitive abdomen. Some arachasae hide this under hollow tree stumps, while other, "armored" varieties exist which have grown a thick carapace that covers all the more delicate parts of their bodies.

Barghest

"Shun sin! Renounce foul deeds! And if evil threatens to overwhelm your will, ponder the fate of the Outskirts of Vizima!"

  • Class: Specter
  • Habitat: In packs near roads, usually at night
  • Weakness: Specter Oil, Moon Dust, Axii, Yrden

Folk of simple or superstitious minds claim committing particularly rotten acts will bring down the wrath of the gods in the form of "barghests,".Witchers rarely believe in the gods, but they do accept that barghests exist and are always connected with some sequence of tragic events that happened in the past.

Beann'shies

"Beann'shies howl and shriek, and if you hear their cry, you can know you'll join the ranks of the dead that night.""

  • Class: Specter
  • Habitat: The Duchy of Toussaint
  • Weakness: Specter dust, Infused dust, Lunar shards

Old gossips claim beann'shies are the spirits of women stuck between life and death due to traumatic experiences. Their wails and howls are considered ill omens of imminent, inevitable death, though they are said not to attack the living themselves.

Berserker

"Now finish your soup, or a berserker'll come and swallow ye whole."

  • Class: Cursed One
  • Habitat: Anywhere humans are, more common in Skellige
  • Weakness: Devil's Puffball, Cursed oil

Skellige legends speak of men known as berserkers who transform into bears when overwhelmed by battle rage. In doing so, they lose all self-awareness and are driven by a bloodlust which they must satiate in order to return to human form.

Botchling

"Saying a botchling's ugly is like saying shit's not particularly tasty: can't say it's a lie, but it doesn't exactly convey the whole truth, either."

  • Class: Cursed One
  • Habitat: Anywhere an unwanted child is hastily discarded without a proper burial
  • Weakness: Cursed oil, Axii

Born of dead, unwanted babies discarded without a proper burial, the botchlings' appearance is that of a partially-decayed fetus, their unformed flesh twisted with hate, fear and malice.

Chort

"Chorts are smaller than fiends, true. But still big enough to kill."

  • Class: Relict
  • Habitat: Cave burrows
  • Weakness: Devil's Puffball, Relict Oil

Chorts fight with little finesse, running straight towards their opponent and trying to knock him to the ground with the force of their charge. After downing their foe they bite, kick and strike with their claw-tipped paws.

Cockatrice

"Had meself eight heifers, five of 'em milchers. Then this cockatrice sprung up nearby, and now all's I got left's dried patties and an empty field."

  • Class: Draconid
  • Habitat: Caves and Dark Cellars
  • Weakness: Grapeshot, Aard

Foolish superstitions claim cockatrices, like basilisks, can kill with their gaze alone. That is utter nonsense, however. One should instead watch out for its sharp beak and long tail, which it can whip to murderous effect.

Crones

"Sister crones, hand in hand, terrors of the sea and land, thus do go about, about: thrice to thine and thrice to mine, and thrice again, to make up nine."

  • Class: Relict
  • Habitat: Crookback Bog
  • Weakness: Relict Oil, Quen

The isolated corners of our world harbor creatures older than humans, older than academies and mages, older even than elves and dwarves. The Crones of Crookback Bog are such creatures. No one knows their true names, nor what breed of monstrosity they in fact are.

Cyclops

"How about this... we take a big stake, sharpen its tip, jam it in the cyclops' eye - then sneak out of the cave disguised as sheep. How's that not a good idea?"

  • Class: Ogroid
  • Habitat: Wherever they please, usually somewhere inconvenient
  • Weakness: Ogroid Oil, Quen, Axii

Cyclopses can easily be recognized by the single eye located in the center of their foreheads. If for some reason that is not visible, other tell-tale signs are their enormous size, incredible strength and a seething hatred for all humans.

Drowner

"When at he water's edge, you gotta be quiet. First of all, so as not to scare the fish. Second - so you don't attract drowners."

  • Class: Necrophage
  • Habitat: Beaches and bodies of water
  • Weakness: Igni

A drowner resembles a corpse dredged from the bottom of a pond. It is sickly blue or green in color, with slime and sludge oozing out of every pore and the acrid stench of rot wafting off of it.

Earth Elemental

"How to survive an encounter with an earth element? Simple. Run. Fast as you can."

  • Class: Elemental
  • Habitat: Elven ruins
  • Weakness: Dimeritium, Elementa oil

Earth elementals are made of mud, clay, sand and rock dust clumped together with water and brought alive with magic. While seemingly slow and ponderous, these creatures are nevertheless dangerous and should be avoided at all costs.

Ekimmara

"Bah, ain't nothing to fear. Ekimmaras, why, they ain't nothing more than overgrown bats."

  • Class: Vampire
  • Habitat: Ruins, abandoned villages
  • Weakness: Devil's Puffball, Igni

While ekimmaras do bear a strong resemblance to overgrown bats, they are not nearly as easy to dispatch. They tear their victims to shreds using long, sharp claws and then slurp the splattered blood off the ground.

Endrega Warrior

"Scared of these endregas, are you? Just wait till you see the warriors."

  • Class: Insectoid
  • Habitat: Forests, abandoned villages
  • Weakness:

An endrega warrior's main weapon is its long tail, which is tipped with a club-like growth and spiked with venomous quils. Powerful abdominal muscles allow it to swing this tail with enough force to kill most lesser opponents in one blow.

Ethereal

"The human mind is as wild and unexplored a place as any land far beyond the seas. It is inhabited by manifold dangers - fears, painful memories, traumas. These lurk on the edge of our consciousness, like predators waiting for a moment of weakness in which to reveal themselves... and destroy us."

  • Class: Specter
  • Habitat: The von Everec Estate
  • Weakness: Specter Oil, Blizzard

"The Ethereal resembled Olgierd in appearance and also fought using a technique similar to his. A group of six such creatures lay in wait for Geralt, yet at first only one engaged him in battle.

Fiend

"I regret to inform Your Grace that Your Grace's son fell while hunting a fiend. He died on the spot, along with his squire, his guide, the beaters, his peasant entourage and his hounds."

  • Class: Relict
  • Habitat: Swamps and Bogs
  • Weakness: Relict Oil, Samum, Devil's Puffball

Fiends are walking mountains of muscle capped with horned, tooth-filled heads. When possible they avoid humans, but when not possible, they kill them, and without much difficulty.

Fire Elemental

"The first fire elemental was created by Ransant Alvaro. Sadly, flames engulfed his entire laboratory, burning it - along with every other building on his block - to ash."

  • Class: Elemental
  • Habitat: Magical Ruins
  • Weakness: Dimeritium, Northern Wind, Aard

Fire is the most destructive of the elements, thus the aggressive lethality of the creature that embodies its essence should come as no surprise. Fire elementals are forged in complicated magic rituals for one purpose: destruction. And they pursue this with murderous determination.

Foglet

"If night ever catches you in the swamps, stay put and wait for dawn, even if it means standing waist deep in water with leeches crawling down your trousers."

  • Class: Necrophage
  • Habitat: Swamp fog
  • Weakness: Moon Dust, Quen

Foglets have powerful arms and claws like Zerrikanian kinjals, yet what makes them truly dangerous is their mastery of deception, beguilement and disorientation.

Forktail

"Forktails... Bah! Fuckers' tails're more like cleavers."

  • Class: Draconid
  • Habitat: Mountaintops snd other high places
  • Weakness: Golden Oriole, Grapeshot, Aard

Forktails owe their quaint name to the long sharp growths at the tip of their tails. A blow from this weapon can slice an oaken shield in two – along with the arm that was carrying it.

Gargoyle

"It just me, or is that gargoyle ogling us?"

  • Class: Elemental
  • Habitat: Abandoned Ruins and Castles
  • Weakness: Dimeritium, Quen

Gargoyles are stone statues brought to life by magic in order to guard mages' laboratories and lairs from intruders. Their appearance alone has scared off more than one prospective burglar. Those who do not take fright at the sight of these horned and winged monstrosities usually die shortly thereafter, torn to shreds by stony claws.

Ghouls usually feed in small groups, at times led by an alghoul. Since they delight most in the taste of fresh carcasses, they appear wherever newly-dug graves are to be found: cemeteries, crypts, catacombs and battlefields.

Giant Centipede

"Among the fascinating flora and fauna of the duchy of Toussaint, one also encounters creatures of the decidedly unpleasant sort, such as giant centipedes."

  • Class: Insectoid
  • Habitat: Rural Toussaint
  • Weakness:Insectoid Oil, Yrden

Giant centipedes are able to burrow into the ground with shocking speed, only to then appear back on the surface in another place. Once they select a target, they will circle it determinedly, trying to get close enough to deliver a blow.

Godling

"[Godlings] are always the first to go. Such is the price we pay for civilization's forward march."

  • Class: Relict
  • Habitat: Burrows, moss-covered stumps
  • Weakness: Don't attack Godlings

Godlings are similar to children in behavior and appearance, and, like children, delight in mischief. They watch over people as well as animals, but, shy creatures by nature, the try to do so while remaining sight unseen.

Golem

"If want [sic] us to break through this wall, we're gonna need twenty sappers, seven mules and a hundredweight of saltpeter. Or one golem."

  • Class: Elemental
  • Habitat: Elven ruins and caves
  • Weakness: Dimerituim, Elementa oil

Defeating a golem is extraordinarily difficult: for obvious reasons it does not bleed, it feels no fear or mercy and it is invulnerable to fire and poison. The monster's only weakness is acid.

Grave Hag

"Burying bodies out in the fields, why, it's like laying out a welcome mat for monsters! Best case scenario, some ghouls will sniff them out, eat their fill and be on their ghoulish way. But if, gods forbid, a grave hag takes to feeding at your cemetery, you'll have no end of trouble."

  • Class: Necrophage
  • Habitat: Battlefields and Graveyards
  • Weakness: Black Blood, Yrden, Quen

Grave hags feed on human corpses and in particular on the rotten marrow which they slurp from human bones using their long, prehensile tongues. Once a hag has devoured all corpses within reach, she turns to killing men and burying them in the cemetery as she waits for them to decompose.

Griffin

"Aye, half-eagle, half tomcat, just like on the lord's crest. 'Cept this 'un was carryin' me dead cow 'stead of a scepter."

  • Class: Hybrid
  • Habitat: Mountains, villages
  • Weakness: Grapeshot, Aard

Griffins mate for life and when their partner is attacked they will defend it to the death. For this reason they are often considered the embodiment of courage, loyalty and fighting spirit.

Harpy

"Most monsters don't actually keep any treasure in their lairs. Harpies, though - they like shiny things."

  • Class: Hybrid
  • Habitat: High Cliffs and Rocky Ravines
  • Weakness: Grapeshot, Aard

Harpies hunt in flocks consisting of a handful to up to twenty individuals. Though rather cowardly and cautious, harpies fiercely defend their nests and will not hesitate to attack when outnumbering their foes.

Higher Vampire

"Men, the polite ones, at least, would call me a monster. A blood-drinking freak."

  • Class: Vampire
  • Habitat:Towns and Villages
  • Weakness: Vampire Oil, Igni

Higher vampires are, much more similar to humans than their those bat-like blood slurping cousins. They not only resemble us in appearance, but also share our intelligence and behavioral patterns.

Hound of the Wild Hunt

"Baying at the heels of the Wild hunt are its Hounds, fierce beasts which follow it like dust clouds trailing after a comet. Hushed legends speak of them losing their way at times and descending from the night sky to earth, cold and death following in their wake."

  • Class: Elemental
  • Habitat: Always together with the Wild Hunt
  • Weakness: Dimeritium, Igni, Axii

The Hounds' chief weapons are their claws and teeth. Their battle tactics rely on knocking their opponents to the ground before tearing them to shreds. Badly wounded Hounds fall into a kind of frenzy that adds to their deadly might.

Hym

"Some men have got good reason to fear their own shadows."

  • Class: Specter
  • Habitat: Attached to those who feel a terrible guilt
  • Weakness: Moon dust, Igni

These wraiths only latch onto particularly despicable individuals who have committed some unspeakable crime. To all others, they remain completely invisible. When they do show themselves to the one they torment, they appear as a shadow-clad, humanoid silhouette with long, sharp claws.

Ice Elemental

"I thought to myself - what's a hunk of ice doing in the middle of some lab? And then that hunk of ice got up and broke my legs."

  • Class: Elemental
  • Habitat: Wherever the mage who created it decides
  • Weakness: Dimeritium, Elementa Oil

An ice elemental is a mass of frozen water animated by magic. Deprived of consciousness or independent will, this elemental is boundlessly obedient to the orders of the mage who created it. Those orders usually contain but one syllable: kill.

Ice Giant

"Fled one time in my life. From the Ice Giant. And know what? I'm not a bit ashamed. "

  • Class: Ogroid
  • Habitat: The Isle of Undvik
  • Weakness: Ogroid Oil, Quen

This powerful, primeval monster is quite possibly the last of its race. The Ice Giant resembles a man in many respects, but is blue as frost and taller than a tree.

Ice Troll

"Our winter's cold and deep, frostbite eats your feet, snow falls down and hides the ground and ice trolls wake from sleep!"

  • Class: Ogroid
  • Habitat:High mountain ridges
  • Weakness: Ogroid Oil, Quen

Ice trolls are crueler than their rocky kin. Though they use similar tactics in battle – tossing stones and swinging with their mighty fists – they are heartier and stronger than rock trolls and thus more dangerous.

Katakan

"Melitele, Great Mother, protect us from evil, from unclean devils and foul demons, and most of all from the clutches of katakans and nekurats..."

  • Class: Vampire
  • Habitat: Ruins and Abandoned Villages
  • Weakness:Moondust, Yyden, Igni

Katakans hide in the shadows and feed on blood. They resemble enormous bats – though with long fangs and even longer talons. And, as if that weren't terror enough, they can turn invisible, waiting unseen while dread of their unpreventable attack overwhelms their victim.

Leshen

"We never hunt in these woods. Never. Even if it means the whole village starves."

  • Class: Relic
  • Habitat: Dense primeval woods
  • Weakness: Relict Oil, Dimeritium Bomb, Igni

Leshens use their inborn magic to control the plants and animals in their territory. Those old enough to earn the appellation "ancient" wield advanced skills and tactics that make them particularly dangerous.

Nekkers

"Take heed, gents, there's nekkers under this here bridge. If you all cross at once, without slowing or stopping, there's nothing to fear. But if your cart throws an axle and you get stuck out there... Well, close your eyes and pray to Melitele."

  • Class: Ogroid
  • Habitat: Dark damp woods
  • Weakness:Northern Wind, Ogroid Oil

A lone nekker is harmless. Five are dangerous. Ten can kill even a veteran monster slayer. Particularly troublesome are the larger, stronger individuals known as warriors, as well as the rare breed of nekkers known as phoocas.

Nightwraith

"Nightwraiths exude this immense sadness, this helpless wraith... I fear them, same as anyone. But most of all I feel sorry for them."

  • Class: Specter
  • Habitat: Rural areas at night
  • Weakness: Moon dust, Yrden

Like noonwraiths, nightwraiths are only found in rural areas. Travelers fall victim to them most often, but if legends are to be believed they also sneak into huts at times and murder peasants in their sleep.

Noonwraith

"Despite what is commonly thought, peasants do not interrupt their labors at midday to get out of the sun - they do it to avoid noonwraiths."

  • Class: Specter
  • Habitat: Rural areas at high noon
  • Weakness: Specter Oil

Crops swaying in the fields on a windless day indicate that a noonwraith is nearby. These monsters kill humans who venture into the fields at noontime.

Pale Widow

"Never have I seen such a creature! It was like a giant centipede, but white, utterly white!"

  • Class: Insectoid
  • Habitat: Deep underground
  • Weakness: Insectoid Oil, Yrden

This rare variety of giant centipede dwells in underground lairs and owes its name to its unusual, pale white coloring. Like the more common giant centipede, its body is covered in hard chitinous armor, with numerous hooked limbs sticking out from underneath.

Penitent

"I run back inside, hasp the doors, and then I hear it - someone whispering my name. Mikkjaaal, Mikkjaaal! "

  • Class: Specter
  • Habitat: Eldberg Lighthouse
  • Weakness: Specter Oil, Moon dust, Yrden, Quen

Wherever this wraith haunts immediately becomes enveloped in thick fog and darkness - and those that wander into it usually never return.

Plague Maidens

"Patients seem to have hallucinations of a woman covered in scabs and boils, with rats scurrying about all around her. These ravings subsided after an administration of henbane and poppy extract."

  • Class: Specter
  • Habitat: Near plague-ridden communities
  • Weakness: Specter Oil, Yrden

As the name "plague maiden" suggests, these wraiths take the appearance of females, though exactly why that is remains a mystery. Some speculate they, like other such specters, arise from the powerful emotional charge associated with certain circumstances of death, such as death preceded by a long and particularly painful illness.

Rock Troll

"Man must riddly talk. But no tricksy. Or troll boom man head. "

  • Class: Ogroid
  • Habitat: High, rocky mountains
  • Weakness: Orgoid, Quen

Trolls are able to use fire and simple tools, and some of them have even mastered the basics of Common Speech. Though linguistic nuances such as conjugations and declinations escape them, they are extremely fond of riddles, rhymes and all sorts of wordplay.

Rotfiend

"Course it reeks. Think they're called rotfiends because they smell like roses?"

  • Class: Necrophage
  • Habitat: Battlefields and disease-stricken areas
  • Weakness: Necrophage Oil

Rotfiends resemble decomposing human bodies that have been stripped of their skin. Their presence is given away by the overwhelming stench of the rot which gives them their name. Devourers are a particularly dangerous kind of rotfiend marked by an insatiable appetite for human flesh.

Siren

"Out at sea, if you hear a beautiful woman singing, turn the ship around at once. You understand? Even if it means sailing straight back into a storm."

  • Class: Hybrid
  • Habitat: The coasts of the North Sea
  • Weakness: Grapeshot, Igni, Aard

Once a naive sailor gets within arm's reach of these beautiful creatures, their fair faces suddenly turn to fang-filled, fish-like maws, and lovely tails promising unknown delights become sharp, death dealing talons.

Shaelmaar

"The earth's innards are home to mighty strange and fearsome beasts. Were they ever to decide to ramble up to the surface, we'd all be gutted the very first day o' the invasion. "

  • Class: Relict
  • Habitat: Deep undergound
  • Weakness: Relict Oil, Samum, Aard

Since shaelmaars are blind, they find their way by sensing vibrations and listening for sounds. Thus the best tactic when fighting such a monster is to hurl something heavy or noisy against a nearby boulder or wall. Then pray silently that the beast will roll towards the sound and knock itself out upon striking into the obstacle.

Slyzard

"Some peasants once offered me a king's ransom to slay a slyzard. A damned hefty purse, chock full o' gold... But I turned 'em down. Coin's no good if you're dead. And a slyzard, that's no fuckin' forktail."

  • Class: Draconid
  • Habitat: Dragon Mountains
  • Weakness: Grapeshot, Aard, Quen

Slyzards are often mistaken for wyverns or forktails. Yet make no mistake: slyzards are nasty, terribly dangerous beasts, and confusing them for wyverns will end very badly for the confuser. While a wyvern can tear apart and devour an untrained man in seconds, only a slyzard can first bake him to a crisp with a waft of fiery breath.

Spriggan

"I was gathering berries, as one does this time of year, when suddenly the trees shook and I saw this... this thing..."

  • Class: Relict
  • Habitat: Unfrequented woodlands
  • Weakness: Relict Oil, Dimeritium, Igni

Spriggans do not usually attack humans if unprovoked, but when irritated they can be quite fearsome, and, as they possess considerable strength, they are capable of doing great damage. Their appetite is such that they can devour more flesh than an army at a wedding.

Succubus

"Again?! Good grief, woman, I'm spent..."

  • Class: Hybrid
  • Habitat:Towns and Villages
  • Weakness: Hybrid Oil, Quen

Unlike other monsters, succubi and menads feel no desire to kill, do not crave human blood and usually do not, in fact, mean any harm at all. They are motivated by one thing and one thing only: an insatiable lust.

Sylvan

"Sylvans are cruel, greedy and treacherous. Still, I prefer them to dh'oine."

  • Class: Relict
  • Habitat: Deep Woods
  • Weakness: Relict Oil, Samum, Devil's Puffball, Igni

Sylvans and Yakshas, a kindred species, are extremely rare woodland creatures whose appearance combines traits of goats and rotund men. These beings usually pose little danger, for they limit their contact with humans to playing harmless (though often bothersome) tricks and eating crops from their fields.

The Toad Prince

"The girl walked up to the edge of the pond. There, squatting amidst the fragrant lilies and broad lily-pads, she saw a most unusual sight: a small green frog with a golden crown perched on its warty head."

  • Class: Cursed One
  • Habitat: The sewers of Oxenfurt
  • Weakness: Northern Wind, Golden Oriole, Curse oil, Yrden

The Toad Prince prowling the Oxenfurt sewers was actually Prince Sirvat,[1] an heir to the Ofiri throne. He had been inadvertently cursed by Olgierd von Everec out of anger when the parents of Olgierd's love, Iris, intended to give their daughter's hand in marriage to the visiting prince.

Venomous Arachaesae

"Barley nicked me, I'll be fine. "

  • Class: Insectoid
  • Habitat: Forests
  • Weakness: Insectoid Oil, Golden Oriole

Though all arachasae are highly venomous, this breed produces an especially strong toxin. A few drops are enough to kill a grown man - unless that man is a witcher, whose mutations will neutralize small amounts of this venom. Large quantities, however, will kill anything they touch, with mutations only prolonging an inevitable and painful death in such instances.

Water Hag

"Folk say water hags are drowners' wives. If that be true, 'tain't no wonder why they're such ornery bitches."

  • Class: Necrophage
  • Habitat: Swamps
  • Weakness: Northern Wind, Quen, Igni

The body of a Water Hag is wrinkled, wart-covered, and nearly two yards tall, with skin the color of a long-dead cadaver and stinking of muck and fish.

Werewolf

"The baron's body swelled as his muscles grew, his noble attire fell in tatters on the marble floor. 'My beloved, you... you are so hairy. You are a werewolf!'"

  • Class: Cursed One
  • Habitat: Under a full moon
  • Weakness: Moon dust, Devil's Puffball, Cursed Oil, Igni

Neither animal, nor man, the Werewolf takes the worst from both species: the bloodlust and primal nature of a wolf, and the ruthlessness and cruelty of a human.

Wight

"They say the dead like quiet. I don't know about the dead, but wights certainly do."

  • Class: Necrophage
  • Habitat: Ancient Burial Sites
  • Weakness: Necrophage Oil, Axii

Though wights are ghastly and threatening in appearance, one should not approach them with sword drawn or attack them unprovoked. Left alone, they present no serious danger to anyone and are far more interested in mixing noxious brews in cauldrons than in fighting

Wraith

Finish all your business before you die. Bid your loved ones farewell. Write your will. Apologize to those you've wronged. Otherwise, you'll never truly leave this world.

  • Class: Specter
  • Habitat: Near cemeteries at night
  • Weakness: Moon dust, Yrden, Quen

Wraiths are usually encountered at night, near cemeteries, catacombs and other such burial places, or else near the places that were important to them in life: abandoned homes, crumbling castles or forgotten bridges.

Wyvern

  • Class: Draconid
  • Habitat: Anywhere with sky access
  • Weakness: Grapeshot, Aard

Wyverns are often mistaken for dragons, and, though they are much smaller than their more famous kin and do not breathe fire, they arw likwise extremely dangerous monsters.
